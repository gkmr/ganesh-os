The operator behind the system

Ganesh OS is not a hobby project. It is the same job its author does for companies, run at the scale of one life and shipped in public so the work is inspectable. This page makes the leadership explicit: the roles held, the functions led, and the throughline that connects a personal system to a professional track record.

Operator-investor: two roles, one discipline

I invest in AI-native companies, and I build them. Those are not separate careers; they are the same judgment applied from two sides of the table. The investor knows what "real" looks like because the builder has shipped it; the builder prices risk like an investor because he has had to diligence it.

Role 1 — VC partner (invest)

Backing AI-native companies as a partner: sourcing, diligence, and hands-on portfolio support.

  • Sourcing — finding teams building genuinely AI-native products, not AI-flavored wrappers.
  • Technical diligence — separating a working system from a demo: where is the judgment, where is the determinism, and what happens when a model is wrong.
  • Governance diligence — the question almost no one asks yet: when these agents act autonomously, who can audit it, recover it, and stop it? This repo is the reference answer.
  • Portfolio support — product, platform, and GTM help for founders, from someone who has operated those functions at scale.

The edge: an investor who has built the agent-governance layer himself can tell which "agentic AI" is real and which is a slide.

Role 2 — Fractional CPO / CTO (build)

Standing up and scaling the product-and-engineering function for AI-native teams. The functions led:

  • AI / ML product — the model-facing surface: what the system decides, how it explains itself, and where a human stays in the loop.
  • Platform engineering — the substrate underneath the product: coordination, state, memory, and the guardrails that make autonomy safe.
  • Design — the interface as the product. In this system the output is the surface, wireframed before any logic.
  • Go-to-market — taking the thing to revenue: positioning, the first customers, and the motion that scales.

The throughline

Every personal-life problem in this system turned out to be a company problem in disguise, and every fix is one I have shipped in a company before:

  • Coordinating many autonomous actors without clobbering shared state → platform engineering.
  • Deciding where the model judges and where code must be exact → AI/ML product.
  • Making the output legible and trustworthy in one glance → design.
  • Turning a surfacing layer into something people actually act on → go-to-market thinking, applied to a user of one.
  • Keeping all of it auditable, recoverable, and human-gated → the governance thesis I diligence as an investor.

I didn't build a to-do app. I ran the playbook I run for companies — on myself, where every decision is inspectable.

What I'm open to

The active focus is the work above. Concretely, the engagements I take: board & advisory roles, fractional CPO / CTO work, panels & talks, angel investing and diligence, and selective mentoring of founders and senior product/eng leaders — all centered on agent governance, evals, and making autonomous AI safe to run unattended.
